System and Method for Automated Monitoring of Agrarian Property Rights Transitions

Resumen Técnico
===============
Sistema de monitoreo geoespacial automatizado diseñado para rastrear transiciones en los derechos de propiedad agraria, específicamente la conversión de propiedad comunal ("Ejidal") a propiedad privada ("Dominio Pleno"). El sistema interactúa con los registros públicos del Registro Agrario Nacional (RAN) y las gacetas de los tribunales agrarios para identificar parcelas que entran en el proceso de privatización. Al combinar imágenes satelitales de uso de suelo con actualizaciones de estado legal, el sistema identifica oportunidades de "Exploración" (Scouting) de alto valor para el desarrollo inmobiliario en zonas emergentes. El sistema cuenta con un "Clasificador de Riesgo de Conflicto" que identifica parcelas con litigios agrarios no resueltos, protegiendo a los inversores de gravámenes legales a largo plazo. Esto proporciona un puente técnico para acceder a información que tradicionalmente es difícil de recuperar e interpretar, permitiendo la adquisición de tierras en desarrollo basada en datos.

Campo Técnico
=============
This disclosure relates to the field of Real Estate Technology (PropTech), Geographic Information Systems (GIS), and automated legal monitoring for land development.

Antecedentes y Problema Técnico
===============================
A large percentage of land in Mexico is held under the "Ejido" system (communal ownership). This land cannot be sold as private property until it goes through a specific legal process called "Dominio Pleno." Identifying which "Ejidos" are voting to privatize their land is a major advantage for developers, as the land is significantly cheaper before the title is finalized. However, RAN records are notoriously difficult to access digitally, and the process is opaque. There is a need for a system that "scouts" these legal transitions automatically to identify investment frontiers.

Ejemplos de Realización
=======================
In one embodiment, the system utilizes NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) data from satellites to monitor if an "Ejidal" parcel is being cleared of vegetation, which often precedes the formal legal request for privatization, providing a "Pre-Legal" signal of development.

Reivindicación de Novedad
=========================
The system claims novelty in the combination of automated agrarian legal status monitoring (RAN scraping) with satellite-based land-use analysis to predict and track the transition of communal land into private real estate markets.
