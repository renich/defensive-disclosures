Abstract
========
An automated monitoring system designed for the proactive management of vehicle fleet traffic infractions. The system interfaces with multiple municipal and state-level traffic authority portals to perform recurring queries based on license plate numbers and vehicle identification numbers (VIN). A centralized tracking engine identifies new infractions (e.g., speed camera tickets, parking fines) and calculates eligibility for "Early Payment" discounts. The system includes an automated notification workflow that alerts fleet managers and specific drivers, facilitating immediate payment or legal contestation. By ensuring that fines are addressed before incurring late fees or preventing vehicle registration renewals (refrendo/verificación), the system significantly reduces the operational and financial overhead for logistics and transportation enterprises.

Technical Field
===============
This disclosure relates to the field of Fleet Management Systems (FMS), automated government portal interaction, and financial risk management software.

Background & Problem Statement
==============================
For companies managing hundreds or thousands of vehicles, traffic fines are a constant operational risk. Municipalities often do not send physical notices, or notices are lost in transit. By the time a fleet manager realizes a vehicle has a fine (often during the annual "Verificación" or registration renewal), the amount has doubled due to late fees, and the vehicle may be grounded until the debt is cleared. Manually checking dozens of municipal websites for every plate in a fleet is an impossible task.
